**Document Version:** 1.0

**Research Date:** February 27, 2026

## Overview

TELOS is a Greek word meaning "purpose" or "ultimate aim" - the end toward which all actions are directed. PAI's TELOS framework is a comprehensive life/identity framework that helps users articulate their complete self-understanding across multiple dimensions.

**Core Concept:** Users cannot be effectively served by AI unless the AI understands their purpose, beliefs, goals, challenges, and worldview.

---

## TELOS Framework Structure

The TELOS framework consists of 10 key sections:

### 1. MISSIONS (M#)
**Purpose:** Core life missions - the ultimate purposes that drive everything else

**Description:** These are your fundamental life purposes. Not goals (which are bounded), but ongoing missions.

**Example Format:**
```
M0: Activate as many creative people as possible through technology
M1: Build systems that magnify human intelligence, not replace it
M2: Create a sustainable lifestyle that models what I teach
```

**For axi Users:**
- What are you fundamentally trying to achieve?
- What ultimate purposes drive your work?
- What legacy do you want to create?

### 2. GOALS (G#)
**Purpose:** Specific objectives supporting missions

**Description:** Time-bounded, measurable objectives that support your missions.

**Structure:**
- General goals (ongoing)
- Annual/quarterly goals (time-specific)

**Example Format:**
```
G0: Complete PAI v3.0 implementation by Q2 2026
G1: Publish 12 AI/security articles this year
G2: Build and launch my personal AI assistant by March 2026

2026 Goals:
G3: Grow audience to 100K followers
G4: Complete machine learning specialization
```

**For axi Users:**
- What specific outcomes are you working toward?
- What's your timeline?
- How do these goals support your missions?

### 3. CHALLENGES (C#)
**Purpose:** Current obstacles you're working to overcome

**Description:** Be brutally honest about what's holding you back.

**Example Format:**
```
C0: Limited time - too many projects, not enough execution
C1: Technical debt in early projects blocking new features
C2: Inconsistent writing discipline - start strong, fade quickly
C3: Analysis paralysis on major architecture decisions
```

**For axi Users:**
- What's stopping you from achieving your goals?
- What patterns repeat?
- Where do you struggle most?

### 4. STRATEGIES (S#)
**Purpose:** Approaches to addressing challenges and achieving goals

**Description:** Your proven and experimental strategies.

**Example Format:**
```
S0: Time blocking - dedicated 4-hour work blocks for deep focus
S1: Breaking large projects into small, shippable units
S2: Weekly review + monthly retrospective cycle
S3: Recruiting accountability partners for consistency
```

**For axi Users:**
- What approaches work for you?
- What have you tried and abandoned?
- What's your execution philosophy?

### 5. PROBLEMS (P#)
**Purpose:** Problems in the world you want to solve

**Description:** World-scale problems you're passionate about addressing.

**Example Format:**
```
P0: AI is being deployed without adequate safeguards
P1: Most people believe they have no valuable contributions
P2: Knowledge isn't being organized for practical application
```

**For axi Users:**
- What problems are you passionate about?
- Why do these matter to you personally?

### 6. NARRATIVES (N#)
**Purpose:** Active talking points and key messages

**Description:** How you describe yourself, your work, and what matters.

**Example Format:**
```
N0: I help people become the best versions of themselves through AI
N1: AI should magnify everyone, not just the elite
N2: Your identity is the foundation of everything you build
N3: Clear thinking comes before good prompts
```

**For axi Users:**
- How do you describe what you do?
- What's your elevator pitch?
- What messages do you repeat most?

### 7. BELIEFS (B#)
**Purpose:** Core beliefs that guide your decisions and worldview

**Description:** Non-negotiable truths you operate from.

**Example Format:**
```
B0: Excellence compounds - small improvements done consistently beat brilliant one-time efforts
B1: Transparency builds trust faster than anything else
B2: Systems matter more than individual genius
B3: Everyone has valuable contributions; most just don't know it yet
```

**For axi Users:**
- What fundamental truths guide your decisions?
- What would you never compromise on?
- What do you believe about human potential?

### 8. FRAMES (FR#)
**Purpose:** Useful perspectives for seeing the world

**Description:** Mental models and reframes that help you navigate situations.

**Example Format:**
```
FR0: "Problems are information" - every obstacle reveals something about the system
FR1: "Responsibility is empowerment" - taking ownership is the path to influence
FR2: "Constraints breed creativity" - limitations force innovation
```

**For axi Users:**
- How do you reframe challenges?
- What perspectives help you see clearly?
- What mental models guide your thinking?

### 9. MODELS (MO#)
**Purpose:** Your understanding of how things work

**Description:** Explanatory models of systems, behavior, and success.

**Example Format:**
```
MO0: Success = clear goal + sustained effort + course corrections (not talent)
MO1: Organizations fail on culture before they fail on strategy
MO2: Learning happens through doing, not consuming content
MO3: Human motivation peaks when autonomy + mastery + purpose align
```

**For axi Users:**
- How do you understand success?
- What drives human behavior in your field?
- What patterns do you see repeating?

### 10. TRAUMAS (TR#)
**Purpose:** Formative experiences that shaped you

**Description:** Significant experiences (positive and negative) that define your patterns.

**Example Format:**
```
TR0: Raised by immigrant parents who valued education and achievement
TR1: Early business failure taught me the difference between ambition and execution
TR2: Public speaking anxiety from middle school experience
TR3: Breakthrough moment: realized most limitations are self-imposed
```

**For axi Users:**
- What experiences shaped your worldview?
- What patterns come from these experiences?
- Which have you integrated, which are still limiting?

## TELOS Interview Flow

A structured interview guides users through TELOS discovery. This is not a one-time event but an iterative refinement process.

### Interview Design

**Format:** Conversational Q&A with 5-7 questions per section

**Session Structure:**
1. Introduction (explain framework)
2. 10 sections × 5-10 min each = 60-90 min total
3. Can be done in one session or multiple sessions
4. Results stored in MEMORY/USER/TELOS.md

**Example Interview for MISSIONS:**

**Q1:** "If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about the world, what would it be?"
- Captures core passion

**Q2:** "What do people most often ask for your help with?"
- Reveals natural strengths

**Q3:** "Looking back on your life, what have you been consistently working toward?"
- Identifies recurring themes

**Q4:** "What would you do if money and status weren't concerns?"
- Separates intrinsic from extrinsic motivation

**Q5:** "What do you want to be remembered for?"
- Connects to legacy

**Q6:** "When do you feel most alive and engaged?"
- Identifies flow states

**Q7:** "What's one thing nobody knows about your true ambitions?"
- Uncovers hidden goals

### Interview for CHALLENGES:

**Q1:** "What keeps you up at night?"
- Identifies persistent worries

**Q2:** "When do you most often feel frustrated?"
- Reveals friction points

**Q3:** "What patterns do you keep repeating despite wanting to change?"
- Surfaces recurring patterns

**Q4:** "What would you attempt if you knew you couldn't fail?"
- Identifies fear-based limiting beliefs

**Q5:** "What's one area where you consistently underperform your potential?"
- Captures reality vs aspiration gap

**Q6:** "What external factors most limit your progress?"
- Distinguishes real vs perceived constraints

**Q7:** "What would solving your biggest challenge require you to change about yourself?"
- Links challenges to identity work

### Interview for GOALS:

**Q1:** "What do you want to accomplish in the next 90 days?"
- Near-term focus

**Q2:** "What's your 1-year vision for your work/life?"
- Medium-term direction

**Q3:** "Where do you want to be in 5 years?"
- Long-term trajectory

**Q4:** "How would you measure success in each area?"
- Forces specificity

**Q5:** "What's one goal you've wanted to pursue but haven't?"
- Uncovers hidden ambitions

**Q6:** "What would achieving your goals require of you?"
- Links goals to identity/capability growth

### Implementation for axi Users

**Phase 1: One-Time TELOS Interview**
- Conversational flow through 10 sections
- 60-90 minute session
- Results saved to MEMORY/USER/TELOS.md

**Phase 2: Quarterly TELOS Review**
- Update goals and challenges quarterly
- Refresh strategies based on learnings
- Track mission progress

**Phase 3: TELOS Integration in Decisions**
- When user asks for advice, axi refers to their TELOS
- Capability selection considers TELOS alignment
- Recommendations grounded in user's stated values

## Integration Guide for axi

**When selecting capabilities:**
- Consider user's stated problems and challenges
- Recommend tools aligned with user's proven strategies
- Avoid capabilities that conflict with user's values

**When advising:**
- Reference specific TELOS elements in recommendations
- Ground suggestions in user's stated beliefs and goals
- Link proposed actions to user's missions

**When reflecting:**
- Ask about progress toward stated goals
- Identify new challenges emerging
- Update TELOS quarterly

---

## Connection to PAI Algorithm

**OBSERVE Phase:**
- If user referenced their own capabilities/constraints, refer to TELOS
- Use TELOS beliefs to inform capability audit

**THINK Phase:**
- Pressure test assumptions against user's stated values/beliefs
- Consider user's known challenges when evaluating approaches

**PLAN Phase:**
- Recommend strategies aligned with user's proven approaches (TELOS/S#)
- Consider user's challenges (TELOS/C#) in planning

**BUILD/EXECUTE/VERIFY:**
- Execute using user's preferred strategies
- Monitor for alignment with stated goals

**LEARN Phase:**
- Update TELOS if learnings reveal shifts in beliefs/understanding
- Track goal progress against TELOS/G#
